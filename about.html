About This Project

What The Project Is About

This is a collaborative project based on the brief: “The past, present, or future of Manchester”.

We were also required to choose a “SODA theme”. These include: Society, Body, Storytelling, and Machine. Our choice was “Storytelling”, as we felt this would allow for more creative expression.

Our overall chosen topic was “Urban Legends in Manchester”, centred on the past of this city. The legend that piqued our interest the most was “The White Lady of Ordsall Hall” due to her compellingly sad story.

Initial Research

We began by individually choosing some urban legends that interested us, and then coming together to decide on one that we thought would be the most exciting to work on.

Findings At Ordsall

Dan and I (Chloe) took a trip to Ordsall Hall in early March 2024 to see if we could find some information on Margaret Radclyffe, the White Lady herself. (Have a look at their website to learn more about The White Lady)

We were able to speak with a very helpful member of staff, who gave us a tour of the building and told us some very interesting points. As we know, Margaret died of a “broken heart” shortly after her brother’s death; this was an official diagnosis. Margaret was part of the Queen’s “entourage”, and following her death, the Queen ordered an autopsy on her (which was actually illegal at the time, and considering Margaret’s family was Catholic, they wouldn’t have agreed to this). They found fatty strings on her heart, indicating that she starved herself, and leading to her tragic diagnosis.

We were also able to find out that it was her brother who bought her notable white dress for her. Their bond was seemingly very strong.
